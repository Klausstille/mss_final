    What We Offer

    Clothing

    MSS specialises in high quality design developments and flexible production runs with low MOQ for the real world fashion designers and brands. we provide services in tailoring, dress making, pattern making, cut and sew and knitwear. we are comfortable working with most materials such as organic or synthetic woven fabrics and jersey, leather and simulated fur…etc. in terms of printing and embellishments, please refer to our designate section.

    Jewellery

    MSS provides exquisite skills and craftsmanship to ensure each piece is produced with the finest qualities. We handle a big range of materials ranging from silver, brass, stainless steel, zinc alloy, tin alloy to resin, acrylic, wood, horn and leather. we can also work with precious metals on request. We are experienced with various techniques such as 3D printing, mould making, die-cut, laser cut, stone setting, enamelling, hand polishing, assembling…etc. We have our own plating consultant that works with our plating partners to provide perfect solutions to all plating requirements. We have an in-house testing laboratory for testing any possible irritable substances. As for our quality control, we have a hundred percent end-to-end inline control to ensure every piece is made to perfection.

    Who We Are

    Missing Sock Studio was founded in 2000 by Ahlaiya Yung and Nana Aganovich, graduates of London’s prestigious Central Saint Martins school. Originally based in Shanghai the facility relocated to Shenzhen in 2002 to benefit from the proximity of Hong Kong’s fabric market and its strong logistical and international business network.
    Missing Sock’s USP was immediately evident in the fact that its founders were both designers rather than factory owners. A designer’s priority will always be quality rather than profit. Their quest for quality led them to make an unusual adjustment to the traditional production process. Rather than opting for the more economical assembly line format they chose to train their seamstresses to construct an entire garment.
    Initially this decision was simply the result of a desire for superior quality but it quickly developed into the core ingredient of the company’s mission statement.

    A self-styled ‘aesthactivist’, Ahlaiya Yung has cultivated a career of implementing social change through aesthetics and ethics. From hospitality (Chez Moi, Salon No.10 in Hong Kong), through fashion design studio (Missing Sock Studio), fashion retail (Creatures Fashion Repertoire) to interior and product design (Metasus) Ahlaiya has continually sought to address key social and market issues by questioning the conventions and rediscovering core values that are often lost in this age of high speed development. This unique approach has generated a profile of a true 21st century entrepreneur. Ahlaiya and Nana soon found that the ‘whole garment’ method fostered skill, encouraged learning and most importantly generated a healthy competitive spirit and attention to detail. What began as nothing more than a tactical decision had unexpectedly evolved into a manifesto: a happy and respected craftsman or woman makes better clothes. Since 2009, Ahlaiya has taken sole ownership of Missing Sock and he has cultivated the studio to a standard that has been able to launch the career of his friend and former colleague Nana Aganovich as well as producing collections for a number of independent and experimental European designers: Mother of Pearl, Unconditional...
